Russian attack on Odesa damages critical infrastructure, leaving residents without power, water for days

Author: Kristina
Published: December 12, 2025
Updated: December 14, 2025


Residential building damaged by a Russian attack on Odesa, Dec. 12, 2025.

Overview

A Russian airstrike on Odesa on Dec. 12, 2025 damaged critical infrastructure and left parts of the city without electricity and water, according to Serhiy Lysak, head of the Odesa Military Administration.

Emergency response

Emergency and utility services were deployed immediately to assess and repair the damage.

Residents later received notifications from energy companies stating that electricity supply is expected to be restored by December 16.

Meanwhile, technical water is being delivered to residents left without running water, according to the State Emergency Service of Ukraine.


Emergency responders dispensing technical water for residents in Odesa, Dec. 13, 2025.

Casualties and broader impact

On Dec. 13, four people were injured in Russian strikes across Odesa Oblast.
Governor Oleh Kiper described the overnight attack as one of the largest since the beginning of the war.

Russia has repeatedly targeted civilian energy and utility infrastructure across Ukraine.
